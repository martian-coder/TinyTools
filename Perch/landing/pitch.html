Investor one-pager · Martian Coders

The parental-safety category is built on surveillance. We built the alternative.

Perch detects grooming, predation, scams, bullying and self-harm signals on a child's phone — on the device itself. Guardians get an instant flag with the reason; the child keeps their privacy; no company ever holds the conversations. Working product, live today.

The wedge

Incumbents (Bark, Qustodio, Aura — $14–99/mo) upload children's private messages to their clouds and scan them there. That model has three structural problems we don't share: a breach honeypot of minors' conversations, a trust ceiling with teens and regulators, and no path into privacy-regulated public-sector distribution. Perch's detection runs where the data already is. Only the conclusion — category, reason, sender name, timestamp — ever crosses the wire. The database schema cannot store message content. That is the pitch, and it is independently auditable because the source is public.

Why now

  • Regulatory tailwind: UK Online Safety Act, EU DSA and child-safety codes, and US state-level minor-safety laws are forcing the "who protects kids online" question — while GDPR-class privacy law punishes exactly the cloud-scanning model incumbents use.
  • Technical unlock: on-device AI (Gemini Nano-class models shipping in Android/Chrome) means serious detection no longer requires a server. The incumbent architecture is now a choice, and a liability.
  • Distribution unlock: a child-safety tool that cannot read messages is the first one a ministry, school system or telecom can endorse without creating a surveillance program.

Business model

Consumer (B2C)

Free core (the moat is trust); premium tier for multi-child households, extended AI briefings and history — undercutting $14–99/mo incumbents at ~$3–5/mo.

Public sector / white-label (B2G)

Per-deployment licensing: national branding, sovereign self-hosted relay, localized detection packs, source audit rights. One ministry contract ≈ years of consumer revenue.

Elder-safety line

The identical engine flags OTP/prize scams on grandparents' phones — a second demographic with the same binary, and strong bank/telco partnership logic.

Detection-as-SDK

License the on-device engine to messengers, keyboards and OEMs who want child-safety features without touching content server-side.

Moat

  • Architecture as positioning: incumbents cannot copy "we never see messages" without destroying their own data pipelines, pricing and stated value proposition.
  • Trust compounding: open source + child-visible transparency is a brand position that strengthens with every incumbent breach headline.
  • Detection corpus: lexicons tuned per language/region with child-safety agencies become a defensible, licensable asset.

Roadmap (next two quarters)

  1. Pilot cohort: 100 families; measure flag precision/recall, tune lexicons; signed Play Store release.
  2. On-device LLM as a second-stage classifier behind the pattern engine (borderline cases), languages beyond English.
  3. iOS guardian app (guardian side is fully feasible on iOS; protected-phone side remains Android-first — stated honestly).
  4. First institutional pilot: one school district or one ministry program, white-label.
