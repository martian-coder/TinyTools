Privacy Policy

Effective 13 July 2026 · applies to the Perch Android app and web app

The one-paragraph version

Perch scans incoming messaging notifications on the protected phone itself. If — and only if — a threat pattern is detected, a flag containing category, reason, source app, sender display name and timestamp is sent to the paired guardian. Message content is never transmitted, never stored off the device, and the reporting database has no column that could hold it. Non-flagged notifications produce no record anywhere off the device.

What Perch processes, and where

On the protected phone (never leaves it):

  • Notification text of watched messaging apps — read in memory, scanned locally, then discarded. Not stored, not logged.
  • A local log of flags raised on this phone, visible to the phone's user.
  • A daily counter of how many notifications were scanned (a number only).

Transmitted to the paired guardian via the relay — the complete list:

  • category and severity of a detected threat
  • reason — a fixed, pre-written explanation string
  • app — e.g. "WhatsApp"
  • sender — the display name shown on the notification
  • at — a timestamp

What Perch never collects

  • Message content, photos, media, voice notes
  • Conversation history or contact lists
  • Location, browsing history, app usage statistics
  • Names, accounts or identities — Perch has no user accounts; pairing uses an anonymous one-time code
  • Advertising identifiers, analytics or tracking of any kind — the app contains no analytics SDK

Consent and transparency

Perch is a parental-consent tool for guardians of minors. Setup runs on the protected phone and explains what is monitored before activation. The phone's user can open Perch at any time to see the watched apps, the scan counter, and every flag ever raised — the identical list the guardian sees. Perch is not designed for, and must not be used for, covert monitoring of adults.

Storage and retention

  • Flag metadata is stored in the relay database (PostgreSQL with row-level security; hosted on Supabase, or self-hosted in institutional deployments), keyed to an unguessable pairing identifier.
  • Unlinking (Settings → Unlink & reset) deletes the pairing; database rules cascade-delete all its flags.
  • Local logs live only on the device and are removed by uninstalling the app.

AI features

"Ask Perch" answers guardian questions using the flag log only — the AI never sees message content because message content does not exist off-device. Depending on configuration, answers are generated by an API key the guardian supplies, a managed proxy, or fully on-device models. With no AI available, a deterministic summarizer answers instead.

Your rights

Under GDPR and similar regimes you may request access, correction or deletion of relayed flag metadata. Because Perch holds no accounts or identities, the practical mechanism is unlinking (immediate cascade deletion) or contacting us with your pairing identifier. Contact: martian.coders.x@gmail.com.
